# CMMC CCA Exam Study Guide - Complete PWA

A comprehensive Progressive Web App for CMMC Certified CMMC Assessor (CCA) exam preparation.

## üéØ Study Modes

### 1. Controls Reference (110 controls)
- All 110 Level 2 controls organized by 14 domains
- Filter by study status (studied/unstudied)
- Filter by implementation type (Policy, Config, Software, Hardware, Mixed)
- Progress tracking saved locally
- Search functionality

### 2. Quiz Mode
- **Multiple Choice**: Match controls to requirements
- **Flashcards**: Flip to reveal answers
- **Type Match**: Identify implementation types
- **Domain Match**: Identify which domain controls belong to
- Configurable question count (10, 20, 50, all 110)

### 3. Ethics & Code of Conduct (12 questions)
- Conflict of interest scenarios
- Cooling off periods
- Confidentiality requirements
- Gift policies
- Role boundaries (CCP, CCA, CCI)

### 4. Gotcha Questions (16 questions)
- Regulatory history dates
- Scoring rules and limits
- POA&M restrictions
- Evidence terminology
- Asset categorization

### 5. Assessor Judgment (12 scenarios)
- Real-world assessment situations
- Scoping decisions
- Evidence evaluation
- Inheritance requirements
- "Reasonable assessor" mindset training

### 6. Deep Dive Reference
- All 110 controls with expandable details
- Quick search across all controls
- Domain-organized accordion view

## Features

- **PWA**: Install on any device, works offline
- **Mobile-First**: Responsive design for phone studying
- **Progress Tracking**: LocalStorage saves your progress
- **No Account Required**: Everything runs locally

*******Biggest tip that has helped me so far:*******

Each domain tells a coherent story.  
Instead of memorizing 110 disconnected facts, you're learning 14 stories:

Domain --- The Story It Tells  
AC --- "Who gets in, and what can they do once they're in?"  
AT --- "Do people know what they're supposed to do?"  
AU --- "Can we prove what happened?"  
CM --- "Is the system configured correctly and controlled?"  
IA --- "Are you who you say you are?"  
IR --- "What happens when things go wrong?"  
MA --- "How do we keep things running safely?"  
MP --- "How do we protect the actual data containers?"  
PE --- "Who can physically touch stuff?"  
PS --- "Can we trust the people?"  
RA --- "What could go wrong and how bad?"  
CA --- "Are we actually doing what we say we're doing?"  
SC --- "How do we protect data moving and sitting?"  
SI --- "Is the system healthy and clean?"
